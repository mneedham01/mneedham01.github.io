Eisler and Adorno on the introduction of music to cinema:
“Sound, in the form of music, gave back to those ‘dead’ photographic images some of the life they lost in the process of mechanical reproduction.”
“The magic function of music ... probably consisted in appeasing the evil spirits unconsciously dreaded. Music was introduced as a kind of antidote against the picture" – to “help the spectator absorb the shock”
From "Narratological Perspectives on Film Music," Claudia Gorbman
