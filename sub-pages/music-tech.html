Porting Gestures to puara-gestures
The Puara Framework, an open source library and toolbox, seeks to lower the high barriers of entry to New Interface for Music Expression (NIME) creation. The most recent update to Puara generalizes the expertise in gestural control of T-Stick performers and designers, utilizing a decades-old knowledge base for the creation, maintenance, and performance of other digital musical instruments. Two new gestures, roll and tilt, were ported into the puara-gestures library from the T-Stick, allowing a broader audience of artists and researchers to access these gestural mappings. This expansion of the Puara library continues the mission of creating tools by building on the legacy of historical NIMEs.

Audio Effects Curriculum + New Features in EarSketch
EarSketch is an educational digital audio workstation that engages middle and high schoolers with musical programming. I wrote educational material on audio effects, integrating examples into the EarSketch website with descriptions based on the cultural uses and physics of sound. I also refactored buggy audio features and implemented a new widget that allows users to preview coded beats, connecting the technical and creative processes of music making.
