Spotify’s “On Repeat” playlist, part of an ever-growing legion of personalized playlists developed by the streaming platform, offers a collection of “songs you love right now,” sourced from your listening habits over the past thirty days. Sometimes I glaze over the songs, mildly surprised at what sounds have been – at least according to the data – defining my past month. When I listen to the playlist, I worry about the algorithm cannibalizing itself: by listening to the songs, I push them more into “On Repeat,” an infinity echo in digital streaming.
